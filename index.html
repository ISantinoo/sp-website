3 Graphic Compositions

Repetition-Anomoly

This composition i created shows repetition, by using the same popcorn buckets over and over, however i also added an anomoly to the repetition in the phone, as this instantly stands out from the popcorn, linking to my caption of please turn off your phone in the cinema.

Emphasis-Colour

This composition i created shows emphasis, specifically by colour, here i kept an even shape pattern throughout, to make it as though the only thing the viewer can take from the composition is the difference in colour between the stars.

Rhythm-Size

This composititon i created shows rhythm, specifically size, i have shown this by making the faces progressively go from small to large, however some people may have a different view and see the faces going from large to small, the shift in size and colour implies movement between the faces changing size and colour.

Reflection

The compositions were really fun to make, especially the popcorn one as it was completely hand drawn and then drawn using figma. This took me a while because I wanted it to be perfect, but after a while, I stopped trying to make it perfect and tried to make it the best I could while also making it creative. The learning tutorials and lectures for this were also very helpful, as they gave a good understanding of how we can draw using figma, including text, compositions, etc. The star composition I wanted to make used only shapes to make the emphasis on colour stand out more. I wanted to use a unique shape like a star to make it different from other compositions. This is also kind of linked to Christmas, as it is December. After all, this composition took a while to come up with the idea, but it was good to make. The last composition, (the faces) took me a while to come up with this idea. Eventually, I knew I would want to use size, but how to do it was a different question. To start off with, I used squares, but I thought they were way too basic, so, instead, I changed the faces as I wanted something drawn again like I did for the popcorn. I started with plain coloured faces but thought if I made them colourful it would make the rhythm better and look a lot more appealing and visually aesthetic. After that, I fully started with the website. At first, I had no clue what to do and was extremely confused. However, the worksheets came and helped a lot. It was like every week I had a new understanding of coding and how to make a website. It wasn't all perfect thinking as I did run into some issues, not only issues, but was stuck on a lot of things, confused about some parts of the coding. For example, resizing my components and parts of the websites is not working properly due to issues with the code. One time, it took like half an hour of help from teachers just to find out that I just didn't put a comma in the right place. Mistakes like that were annoying, but eventually, after some help, I became way better and started to code properly, especially after learning how to do CS.
